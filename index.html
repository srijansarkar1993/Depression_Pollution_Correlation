About This Site

What is it and who is it for?

The visualization attempts to show the correlation between depression prevalence and particle pollution in air across global countries based on data for 2023. This visualization is targeted to individuals with depression (currently or in past), medical practitioners and researchers, pharmaceutical organisations and also for anyone interested in this topic.

Why is it important?

The relationship between air pollution and mental health is a growing area of concern and could have significant implications for public health. Understanding the relationship between air pollution and depression is important for improving public health, reducing the burden of mental illness, and ultimately improving quality of life for individuals and communities affected by air pollution.

How do you look at it?

There are 2 parts to the visualization. In the first part we can see a global map where the user can interact by clicking on any country. In the second part we have a stacked bar chart view, where the upper bars represent the severity of particle pollution in air and the lower bars represent the severity of depression across the countries within a geographical regions.The position of the red dots (TBD) will help to visualize the correlation between air pollution and depression. Users can select the region to view through the dropdown in the top or by selecting a country in the global map.
